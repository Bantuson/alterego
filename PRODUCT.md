# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anonymous, build-in-public creators — starting with one: a
beginner-intermediate Python builder in Tembisa on a 4 GB laptop who
publishes as an alter ego. Context of use: alone, at night or between
takes, moving between designing a persona, processing footage, and
going live. The terminal already works; the studio exists to make the
identity feel like a place, not a flag on a command.

## Product Purpose

alterego lets humans drive content without showing their real face,
voice, or room: geometric face disguise, seeded voice shift, background
replacement, auto-editing, branded rendering — post and real-time. The
studio UI is the local control room for all of it. Success: a user
designs a persona, ships a take, or goes live without touching the CLI,
and trusts at every moment that nothing leaves the machine.

## Brand Personality

Clandestine, precise, alive. The feeling of a private atelier /
mission control at midnight — not a consumer video editor. Three
words: covert, crafted, honest.

## Anti-references

- CapCut / Descript / typical editor UIs: white chrome, timeline-first,
  panel grids, feature buffets.
- OBS: utilitarian settings-tree density.
- Generic SaaS dark mode: gradient buttons, glass cards, hero metrics.
- Anything that reads "AI content tool". This is a HUMAN instrument.

## Design Principles

1. **The identity is the interface.** The face constellation is the
   centerpiece and the state indicator; panels orbit it.
2. **Progressive disclosure, single room.** One page, no routes; each
   verb (identity / studio / live) reveals only its own controls.
3. **Local-only is a feature — say it.** Privacy posture is visible,
   not implied.
4. **Truthful previews.** Visualizations use the same math as the
   pipeline; nothing decorative pretends to be data.
5. **Fail-closed honesty carries to UI.** Warnings (coverage, missing
   face) are loud and specific, never toasts that fade.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard reachable controls, visible focus, ≥4.5:1 body contrast on
dark surfaces, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored (constellation still
renders, ambient motion stops). Single-user local tool; no i18n yet.
